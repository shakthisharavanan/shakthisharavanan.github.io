Past Projects

Traffic sign Detection and Classification

Performed traffic sign detection and classification as the car drives around the WPI university campus. Implemented this with 2 approaches: 1) Convolutional Neural Network (CNN) with sliding window, 2) YOLO.Initially trained a CNN (LeNet architecture) with the German GTSD dataset. Then used a sliding window approach to detect the positions of the traffic signs in the video to draw bounding boxes over them.Second approach was with You-only look once (YOLO) algorithm. The same network performed detection and classification. It was much faster (20 fps on a GTX 1060) than the previous method but sacrificed accuracy.

June 2017 - Jan 2018
Autonomous Driving in a Car simulator

Made a car autonomously drive itself in a simulator. Implemented this with 2 approaches: 1) Supervised Learning 2) Reinforcement Learning. In the Supervised learning method, I used the NVIDIA Self-driving CNN architecture and trained it with images from a front facing camera attached to a car from the Udacity car simulator. It was able to autonomously drive to complete one whole lap. The reinforcement approach was implemented with Asynchronous Advantage Actor-critic (A3C) algorithm. I took a distributed approach by running it on 3 different computers at the same time. The car was able to autonomously navigate one lap at reduced speeds in the TORCS simulator after a day of training.

June 2017 - Jan 2018
Lane Line Detection

Detected lane lines as the car drives along a highway. Initially, I implemented with Canny edge detector and Hough transform. This performed reasonably well on straight roads but failed with curved roads. Then I implemented a more robust method of combining results from RGB and HSV color space and then identifying and fitting lane lines with polynomials. This worked much better than the previous method for changing lighting conditions and curved roads.

June 2017 - December 2017
